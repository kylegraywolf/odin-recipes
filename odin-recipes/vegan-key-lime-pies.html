Vegan Key Lime Pies

Key Lime Pies

Key lime pies are a creamy lime-flavored dessert, typically made with a graham cracker crust topped with whipped cream, and served cold. The dish is named after the small key limes it is ideally made with, due to their stronger aromatic properties.

As the title indicates, this version takes a classic recipe and modifies it for the lactose intolerant, and those of choosing to follow a plant based diet. Worry not however, as this recipe is still creamy, and very limey!

Ingredients - Crust

  • 1 sleeve of graham crackers
  • 1/4 cup vegan butter

Ingredients - Filling

  • 1 cup raw cashews (soak for 6 hours first if you do not have a high-power blender)
  • 3/4 cup full-fat coconut milk, well shaken
  • 1/4 cup coconut oil, melted
  • 3/4 cup key-limes, juiced
  • 2 TBSP lime zest
  • 1/3-1/2 cup agave

Instructions - Crust

  1. Preheat oven to 375F
  2. Line muffin tin with 12 paper liners
  3. Add graham crackers to food-processor; process until you have a fine meal.
  4. Add butter; pulse to combine
  5. Distribute crust evenly among muffin tins; press down with the bottom of a glass to flatten
  6. Bake for 10 minutes, or until golden brown.
  7. Remove and set aside to cool

Instructions - Filling

  1. Add all ingredients to blender; blend until creamy and smooth.
  2. Taste and adjust flavor as needed.
  3. Pour filling into muffin tins; tap on counter to release air bubbles.
  4. Top with a small amount of lime zest and loosely cover pies.
  5. Freeze for 2-4 hours, or until firm.
  6. Let sit for 15 minutes at room temperature before serving.
