Vegan Fruit Cobbler

Cobbler is a dessert usually consisting of a fruit filling poured into a baking dish an covered with a batter, biscuit, or dumpling before being baked. Some cobbler recipes, notably in the American South, resemble a thick-crusted, deep dish pie with both a top and bottom crust.

As the title indicates, this version takes a classic recipe and modifies it for the lactose intolerant, and those of choosing to follow a plant based diet. Worry not however, as this recipe still threatens to put you down for a nap with our without a cup of tea!

Ingredients - Biscuit Topping

  • 1/2 cup unsweetened non-dairy milk
  • 2 oz pitted dates (3-4 Medjool/6-8 Deglet Noor)
  • 1/2 very ripe banana
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract/seeds from 1 vanilla bean
  • 1-1/2 cups old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1-1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp cinnamon

Ingredients - Fruit Sauce

  • 1 cup water/fruit juice
  • 2 oz pitted dates (3-4 Medjool/6-8 Deglet Noor)
  • 2 TBSP lemon juice (1 medium lemon)
  • 1/4 cup old-fashioned rolled oats
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon

Ingredients - Fruit

  • 2 cups fresh/frozen sliced fruit (peaches, apples, etc.)
  • 2 cups fresh/frozen berries (blackberries, raspberries, blueberries or berry blend)

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375F
  2. To start the biscuit topping, combine the non-dairy milk, dates, banana, and vanilla into a bowl. Set aside for 15 minutes for the dates to soften.
  3. Grind the total 1-3/4 cups rolled oats into flour with a blender.
  4. Place the fruit into a medium bowl; if frozen, you do not need to thaw beforehand
  5. For the fruit sauce, combine the water, dates, and lemon juice in a blender. Blend until smooth.
  6. Add the 1/4 cup oat flour and 1/4 tsp cinnamon to the fruit sauce, and blend until smooth.
  7. Add the fruit sauce into the bowl of fruit, and toss. Spread evenly into a pie dish or 8x8 baking pan (no parchment is needed).
  8. For the biscuit topping, combine and whisk the remaining 1-1/2 cups oat flour, baking powder, and 1/2 tsp cinnamon in a medium bowl.
  9. Add the non-dairy milk, dates, banana, and vanilla into a blender. Blend until smooth.
  10. Add the blended wet ingredients into the bowl of dry ingredients. Mix well.
  11. Spread the biscuit topping over the fruit sauce in the baking pan, somewhat evenly, leaving some gaps of fruit in between.
  12. Bake uncovered for 25-30 minutes, or until the topping is lightly brown.
  13. Let cool for 10 minutes before serving.
