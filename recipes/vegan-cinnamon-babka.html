Vegan Cinnamon Babka

Cinnamon Babka

Babka is a sweet braided bread or cake which originated in the Jewish communities of Poland, Russia, and Ukraine. It is prepared with a yeast-leavened dough that is rolled out and spread with a filling such as chocolate, cinnamon, or fruit. It is then rolled up and braided before baking.

It was first popularized by the 1990's sitcom, Seinfeld. In the episode, The Dinner Party, Jerry and Elaine are attempting to purchase a chocolate babka to take with them. Ultimately, they resort to purchasing a cinnamon babka, a "lesser babka" according to Elaine. We must take Elain's assertions with a grain of salt, as cinnamon is clearly the "superior" babka.

Ingredients - Dough

  • 345 g AP flour
  • 125 mL plant milk, warm
  • 112 g vegan butter, room temp
  • 40 g coconut sugar
  • 10 g instant dried yeast
  • 1/4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 pinch salt

Ingredients - Cinnamon Filling

  • 55 g vegan butter, melted
  • 80 g coconut sugar
  • 20 g ground cinnamon

Ingredients - Sugar Glaze (optional)

  • 50 g coconut sugar
  • 30 mL water

Instructions

  1. Line a 4in x 8in (10cm x 20cm) loaf pan with parchment paper.

Instructions - Dough

  1. Combine all dough ingredients into a bowl, and mix until they come together.
  2. Knead for 5-10 minutes, until the dough becomes soft, stretch, and comes away from the side of the bowl. Add milk or flour as necessary to form a smooth ball.
  3. Cover with a damp tea towel, leave in a warm place (~80F) for 1 hour, or until the ball roughly doubles in size.

Instructions - Cinnamon Filling

  1. Add all filling ingredients to a bowl, and mix until it forms a paste.
  2. Allow to cool until it thickens to a spreadable and buttery consistency.

Instructions - Assemble

  1. Dust a clean surface with flour, and roll out dough into a rectangle shape.
  2. Spread the filling on to the dough 1 cm away from the edge.
  3. Starting from the long side of the dough, roll the dough into a tight log, try to keep it tight*.
  4. Use a sharp knife to cut the log in half, length-wise, to form two strips of dough.
  5. Twist the strips together, starting by making an 'X'.
  6. Place the dough into the prepared loaf pan.
  7. Cover with a damp tea towel, and let it rise for 1 hour, or let it rise overnight in the refrigerator.

Instructions - Baking

  1. Preheat the oven to 350F when ready to bake.
  2. Bake 30-35 minutes, or until the outside is golden brown and a skewer can cleanly pierce the middle.

Instructions - Sugar Glaze

  1. While baking, combine sugar and water in a small saucepan over medium heat.
  2. Stir until the sugar has dissolved.
  3. Pour over babka, when it is still hot out of the oven.

*If the dough is too soft to roll and shape, put it in the fridge for 30 minutes.
